ABOUT THE DATA

Temperature:

- Explanation: Temperature represents the ambient heat level at a specific location and time. It affects the dryness of forest vegetation and wildfire risk.

- Value Range: Typically measured in degrees Celsius (°C), values can range from below freezing (0°C or lower) to extremely hot (e.g., 40°C or higher), depending on the climate and season.

Relative Humidity (RH):

- Explanation: RH measures the moisture content in the air relative to its capacity to hold moisture. Low RH indicates dry conditions that can contribute to wildfire risk.

- Value Range: Expressed as a percentage, values range from 0% (completely dry air) to 100% (saturated air). Low RH often falls below 30% in dry conditions.

Wind Speed (Ws):

- Explanation: Wind speed measures how fast the air is moving. High wind speeds can spread wildfires quickly.

- Value Range: Measured in kilometers per hour (km/h) or meters per second (m/s), values can range from calm conditions (0 km/h) to strong winds (e.g., 50 km/h or higher).

Rain:

- Explanation: Rainfall measures the amount of precipitation (rain) that has fallen. Adequate rainfall reduces wildfire risk by moistening vegetation.

- Value Range: Measured in millimeters (mm) or inches (in), values can range from 0 (no rain) to heavy rainfall (e.g., 50 mm or more).

Fine Fuel Moisture Code (FFMC):

- Explanation: FFMC assesses the moisture content of fine fuels like leaves and grass. It indicates their flammability.

- Value Range: FFMC values typically range from 0 (extremely dry) to 100 (saturated moisture).

Duff Moisture Code (DMC):

- Explanation: DMC assesses the moisture content in deeper organic layers beneath the surface. It indicates the flammability of these layers.

- Value Range: DMC values range from 0 (dry) to 100 (saturated moisture).

Initial Spread Index (ISI):

- Explanation: ISI estimates the potential for the initial spread of a wildfire. It considers factors like wind speed, fine fuel moisture, and weather conditions.

- Value Range: ISI values typically range from 0 (low spread potential) to higher values (indicating higher potential for fire spread).

Classes:

- Explanation: "Classes" often represents different categories or types of forest or vegetation. Each class may have varying susceptibility to wildfires.

- Value Range: This depends on the classification system used. It can be categorical, such as "Coniferous" or "Deciduous," or numeric, representing specific classes.

Region:

- Explanation: "Region" denotes the geographic location where data was collected. Different regions have unique environmental conditions affecting wildfire risk.

- Value Range: Categorical, representing specific geographic regions or areas.
